# References

The estimators in tickflow follow these sources.

## Bars

- Lopez de Prado, M. (2018). *Advances in Financial Machine Learning*. Wiley.
  (Tick / volume / dollar bars.)

## Realized volatility

- Andersen, T., Bollerslev, T., Diebold, F., Labys, P. (2003). Modeling and
  forecasting realized volatility. *Econometrica*, 71(2).
- Barndorff-Nielsen, O., Shephard, N. (2004). Power and bipower variation with
  stochastic volatility and jumps. *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, 2(1).
- Zhang, L., Mykland, P., Aït-Sahalia, Y. (2005). A tale of two time scales.
  *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, 100(472).
- Barndorff-Nielsen, O., Hansen, P., Lunde, A., Shephard, N. (2008). Designing
  realized kernels. *Econometrica*, 76(6).
- Barndorff-Nielsen, O., Kinnebrock, S., Shephard, N. (2010). Measuring downside
  risk: realized semivariance.
- Corsi, F. (2009). A simple approximate long-memory model of realized
  volatility. *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, 7(2).
- Parkinson, M. (1980); Garman, M., Klass, M. (1980). Range-based estimators.

## Microstructure

- Roll, R. (1984). A simple implicit measure of the effective bid-ask spread.
  *Journal of Finance*, 39(4).
- Amihud, Y. (2002). Illiquidity and stock returns. *Journal of Financial
  Markets*, 5(1).
- Corwin, S., Schultz, P. (2012). A simple way to estimate bid-ask spreads from
  daily high and low prices. *Journal of Finance*, 67(2).
- Lee, C., Ready, M. (1991). Inferring trade direction from intraday data.
- Easley, D., Lopez de Prado, M., O'Hara, M. (2012). Flow toxicity and liquidity
  in a high-frequency world (VPIN). *Review of Financial Studies*, 25(5).

## Jumps

- Lee, S., Mykland, P. (2008). Jumps in financial markets: a new nonparametric
  test and jump dynamics. *Review of Financial Studies*, 21(6).
